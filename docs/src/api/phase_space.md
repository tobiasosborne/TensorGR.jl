# Covariant Phase Space

The Iyer-Wald covariant phase space formalism for diffeomorphism-covariant Lagrangian field theories. Given a Lagrangian density L, computes the equations of motion, symplectic potential, symplectic current, Noether current and charge, Hamiltonian variation, and Wald entropy integrand. Specialized analytic formulas are provided for Einstein-Hilbert gravity.

## Equations of Motion

Extract the equations of motion from a Lagrangian density by variation with respect to dynamical fields. For Einstein-Hilbert gravity L = R, returns the Einstein tensor.

```julia
L_EH = LagrangianDensity(Tensor(:RicScalar, TIndex[]), [:g], :g, :D, 4)
result = eom_extract(L_EH, :g; registry=reg)
# result.eom is the Einstein tensor G_{ab}
```

## Symplectic Potential

The symplectic potential Theta^a arises from the boundary term in the first variation of the Lagrangian. For Einstein-Hilbert gravity, this is the known boundary term from varying the Ricci scalar.

## Symplectic Current

The symplectic current is the antisymmetrized second variation of the symplectic potential. It is conserved on-shell and defines the symplectic form on the space of solutions.

## Noether Current & Charge

For a diffeomorphism generated by a vector field xi, the Noether current J^a = Theta^a(Lie_xi phi) - xi^a L is conserved. On-shell, it is exact: J = dQ, where Q^{ab} is the antisymmetric Noether charge (Komar 2-form for EH gravity).
